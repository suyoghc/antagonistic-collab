Key Takeaway
  • Debate is valuable; the question is how to structure it well
  • Debate is conditionally valuable — epiphenomenal under correct specification, causally necessary under misspecification
  • Every component carries an implicit prior: computation is neutral, debate favors interpretability, the arbiter favors complexity
  • More LLM coordination is not uniformly better — the arbiter helps complex/similarity models (SUSTAIN best-ever 96%, GCM +13.4pp) but hurts simpler/rule models (RULEX −54.7pp wrong winner), and under misspecification the net effect is negative in both domains
Δ: George assumes debate is generally beneficial. We show it's conditionally beneficial — and that each layer of LLM involvement introduces a systematic bias.
  • Both findings replicate cross-domain — debate value under misspec and arbiter bias are domain-general
  • The arbiter bias is toward complexity, not just similarity — revealed only by the second domain
  • Recovery depends on parameter interpretability, not domain content — the representational-format principle
Δ from cat.: Cross-domain replication elevates from finding to principle. One domain = finding, two domains = principle.
Implications
  • For hybrid AI systems: LLM-computation orchestration is a viable paradigm for automated science
  • For model selection: OED can be augmented with natural-language reasoning about theories
  • For philosophy of science: Adversarial collaboration can be structured and automated
  • For hybrid AI systems: Each LLM layer adds an implicit prior — characterize your biases before deploying. The optimal architecture is not "all layers active" but depends on specification quality.
  • For model selection / OED: Standard EIG fails under misspecification. LLM semantic diagnosis provides parameter estimation OED lacks — but only for linguistically describable parameters. Experiment selection methods carry implicit model-type biases.
  • For philosophy of science: Lakatos emerges without career incentives (agents protect core theories via auxiliary modifications ~85% of the time — the "protective belt" is structural, not psychological). "Impartial" experiment selection carries implicit epistemic values. More structured methodology can degrade convergence.
  • For hybrid AI systems: The implicit prior framework is domain-general, not a categorization artifact. Arbiter bias is toward complexity (more params), not just similarity. Design principle: diversify information channels, not weightings.
  • For model selection / OED: The representational-format principle — recovery depends on parameter interpretability, not domain. Cross-domain replication validates that EIG + debate is the optimal regime under misspecification. Formal diversification (R-IDeA) is antagonistic to diagnosis.
  • For philosophy of science: The coordination paradox replicates: seeking decisive experiments privileges complex theories in both domains. Implicit epistemic values in methodology are structural, not domain-contingent. Falsification dominance and auxiliary shielding are robust features of competitive theory evaluation.
